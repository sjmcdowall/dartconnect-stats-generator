Match Exception Instructions: Approve or Edit the following match reports to remove them from this list. Show Instructions

The following matches have circumstances that APPEAR to violate your Season Settings. Use this tool to identify, review and edit match reports that may compromise league results.

Unsaved Matches: Matches that have started but not fully saved in the scoring app will appear here and the account owner and team captains will receive an email notification to reopen the DartConnect app to complete the save process. This can happen if the device was not connected (or lost connection) to WiFi Internet. Contact the teams involved to coordinate fully saving a match on the tablet or device used.

"Force Closing" Unsaved Matches: To manually move an Unsaved match to the completed log, click the red spyglass to force close it. Note, this may risk losing any set or leg data that was not fully saved. Click the blue spyglass to open the partial match report and review what data might be missing.

Incomplete Matches: Incomplete matches have one or more sets unplayed. Use the blue spyglass to examine the match recap for mistakes or errors. You can move incomplete matches to the completed log by clicking the red spyglass , and then use the gold "Close Match" button on the match edit screen. If necessary, contact the match participants to collect any missing information or score results.

Pro Tip: If your league has a format that is artificially triggering exceptions, utilize the Batch Approve tool in the left control panel to accept matches. Learn More

What is an Unknown Player? (Learn More)

Unknown Players are temporary players created on the tablet during league match play to complete the match. These players were created without an email address, so DC cannot match them to existing player records in the League Portal. Leagues ARE allowed to confirm or create player records that lack an email address.

Pro Tip: Always strive for 100% email addresses to assure the league can directly communicate to all players when required. Do not rely on Captains to pass along schedules, updates and event information.

Division Rankings / League Points

Match Scores are determined by the number of legs won by each opponent, unless it is a multi-set format, in which case the match score is the number of sets won by each opponent.

Division Rankings use league points, NOT the match score, to rank competitors throughout the season

League Points are applied to the match AFTER it is completed and imported into the DC platform. These points are NOT displayed in the scorning app or on DCTV, during live match play. They will be visible in division rankings and on match reports.

Use the following league point options to manage your division rankings. Contact DartConnect if your league has a unique format.

Match Points determine the score of the actual match. To set your ranking criteria for the entire league, go to season settings.

For leagues that do NOT use Match Points to determine team/player rankings.

Some Leagues ALSO award additional points for a particular match result such as a match win or a match sweep. These points affect division rankings.

Note: Bonus points are NOT expected to work properly for leagues that use more than one device to score a match, creating separate match reports. Contact DC support to review options.

    Welcome to League Portal 3.0!
    Released December 2021

    The initial scheduling module was released in August 2021, establishing the foundation of our scheduling tool enabling leagues to reliably create a season schedule in a few easy steps. More importantly, our advanced logic accommodates leagues that have different size divisions to cross-share venues – avoiding common schedule conflicts. We hope that this initial effort simplified the effort of darts true unsung heroes: League Operators.

    However, this release did not only benefit the "heroes". It also provided players with an easy-to-follow schedule format with directions and additional venue information. With the scoring application, we implemented a simple match selection interface to simplify league night match set up.

    With the December release, we have extended the scheduling tool to accommodate Post Season Competition. The solution associated with this release is a temporary one, but we hope you will find it to be an improvement over the legacy process. Read on to learn more with even more to come in 2022!

    But that is not all. We have listened to your feedback and instituted a powerful set of edit tools that can be used both during the scheduling process and after the schedule has been published to address teams that drop out and venue closures.

    Post Season Play: Reality check – but some good news

    The enhancements we have been working on since August have limited our ability to create the post-season schedule tool we originally envisioned. We are aware that many late year, seasonal schedules are in the process of winding down regular season play, and we did not want to limit your option to the legacy process. Especially if players have engaged with the new season match listing screen.

    The good news: All leagues (even those who did not use the regular season schedule) may use this feature for post season competition. Here are the steps:

    1. Ensure that you have the "post season”"option activated in Season Settings – this add the post season navigation tab into the league portal header
    2. Create your post season groups (PSG)
    3. Assign each division to their respective PSG
    4. AFTER the regular season completes, use the eligibility tool to remove the teams and players who do not advance to post season playoffs. This removes ineligible teams and players for the scoring app.
    5. Use the "spy glass" with each PSG to create the first round of playoffs, selecting the respective teams for each match.
    6. After each round, use the eligibility tool to remove teams that do not advance, to create the next round.

    The scoring app will continue to list post season matches for the surviving teams.

    We look forward to evolving this process in 2022!

    Match Building ToolsShow +

    DartConnect offers two options for designing your Division Match Formats:

    Basic is designed for competition formats where a league has a single set or sets with with identical formats.

    Advanced is ideal for team leagues where the competition format can vary appreciable throughout the league match.

    Important Information: Please Read

    For EXISTING LEAGUE CUSTOMERS, the Basic Match Builder is the easiest way to start a new season. As before, DartConnect will copy over the prior season competition format for the new season.

    However, the New Advanced Match Builder (AMB) offers unprecedented customization of the leagues match format. It is primarily designed for leagues with multiple sets, where the competition format, game type and number of players can vary appreciably between sets.

    The player experience in the scoring application has also been enhanced. Now each match contains an explicit list sets, enabling players to select the set they want to play next. The scoring app will provide explicit instructions for that set, assisting new teams with league night.

    Requirements:

    To take advantage of this feature, leagues MUST use the schedule option – it is not compatible with the legacy "calendar" option.

    Important!

    The process of creating a competition format for your first division will be more time consuming than the basic method. DartConnect has developed several copy options, to reduce the repetition. However, you should budget additional time to either prepare or transition an existing season. As before, once the competition format for a season is complete, it is automatically copied for subsequent seasons.

    The good news? As long as you are using the scheduling option for a live season, you CAN switch from Basic to Advanced, at any time. Also, if while exploring the AMB, you determine that it will not work for the league, or you are short on time, you can always revert to the Basic Match Builder, saving your work in the AMB

    Please let DartConnect know if you have a feature request or a competition format that the AMB does not accommodate. Contact DC.

    Advanced Match Builder Options

    The following options are now available for each division, for any '01 game type. Leagues can activate these options during the match build process.

    Sudden Death (Beta): Enables any league to set a limit on the number of turns players are allowed to play in an '01 competition, before they must invoke a "sudden death" procedure (as defined by the league) to determine the winner without continuing the existing ’01 leg. The players bull up (as an example) and select the winner to advance to the next leg or set. This can be configured at the division level, allowing for different thresholds for different levels of darts play.

    Dynamic Handicapping (Beta) for Singles Leagues (only). Enables leagues to modify a player's handicap after each round, based on their performance throughout the match. During the Beta phase, leagues must still set the initial HC, if a HC pre-exists. Currently this feature does not keep track of prior competition handicaps, but this feature will evolve.

    AMB option is not available when using Manual Match Setup for Postseason. To change from Basic to AMB, update your Post Season Management Option in the section below to "Live Bracket" or "PS Scheduler"

    Each division must be assigned a post-season group/bracket/division. If you have not set up your post-season groups yet, you must do this before creating divisions.

    What is a Post-Season Group?

    During the regular season, teams are only allowed to play teams within their own division, UNLESS your league has selected the inter-division play option.

    Once post-season play begins, teams often play other divisions at some point during the playoffs. By creating a post-season group, DartConnect can associate the appropriate divisions (and the corresponding teams) to be in the same post-season listing on each device.

    The default will include ALL teams from the corresponding divisions. Once the regular season concludes, leagues have the option of eliminating teams that are ineligible or fail to advance.

    To ensure you have this set up correctly, run the Portal Post Season report to confirm each division has been assigned to the correct post-season group.

    Example

    During the regular season, a Division A1 team only plays other Division A1 teams.

    However, in the post season, teams from A1 may only play each other initially, but after the division winner is determined, they play other "A" level teams (teams from A2, A3, A4, etc.) in pursuit of the "A Championship".

    Therefore, a post-season group labeled "A" should be created. Then every "A" division (A1, A2, A3, etc.) should be assigned to this Post Season Group. Once post season begins, all "A" teams will be available to play each other.

    Note: Like a division, the corresponding proper match format and game menu must be set up as well. This can be done as a secondary step, to expedite the league set up, but do not forget!

    DartConnect League Post-Season Management

    Post season options:

    • Live Brackets. Set up and manage playoffs with an interactive bracket
    • Match Scheduler. Create and schedule matches round by round
    • Manual Match Setup. Matches are set up manually in the scoring app (will NOT work with the Advanced Match Builder)

    Learn More

    3 Basic Steps to Manage Your Post Season:

    • 1. Create Post Season Groups. Similar to divisions, create a group for each set of teams that play each other in the playoffs. Make sure each group has a programmed match format just like divisions in the regular season. PS group match formats can be either identical or different from division formats.
    • 2. Manage Eligibility (Optional). Use this tool to quickly identify which teams/players are allowed to participate based on your league's participation requirements. Recommended for multi-division leagues with a large number of teams and players.
    • 3. Confirm Eligible Teams & Players. Create the final list of teams and players that will participate. Ineligible teams and players are removed from the scoring app.

    If using the Live Bracket or Match Scheduler options, you must continue with the following:

    • 4. Create PS Matches. Each of the above options will create a schedule of matches available to play in the scoring app, but have different methods for updating and managing the competition. The live bracket option features more automation than the match scheduler.
    • 5. Run Optional Venue Report (Match Scheduler Only). Run this report after each round has been created for ALL post season groups to assist in identifying potential conflicts.
    • 6. Notify Players. Send an email to inform players about playoff schedules, eligibility and instructions. Create a custom message with league-specific announcements and updates.
